Why this study exists

Capital is reviewed in rooms. Operations fail in the field.

Transactions, refinancings and major capital programmes are examined by capable legal, financial and technical teams. Yet the most expensive surprises often sit between those workstreams: maintenance that does not match the model, contracts that do not match the operation, fragile succession, regulatory exposure, or a throughput assumption nobody has reconciled against recent performance.

This study is testing whether owners and capital providers need a short, independent operating read before committing further capital, or when an existing asset begins to drift away from the original case.

We are trying to find out where operating reality enters the investment decision, who is trusted to bring it in, and what that judgement is worth.

What the diagnostic examines

01 · Cash and runway

Have real bills and covenant tests been reconciled against the model?

02 · Ground truth

Has an independent operator recently walked the asset and spoken to the people running it?

03 · Model versus operation

Do throughput and utilisation assumptions match recent operating performance?

04 · Decision governance

Are the key risks, contracts, conditions and decisions brought together in one accountable view?
